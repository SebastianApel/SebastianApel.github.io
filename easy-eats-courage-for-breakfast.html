Easy eats Courage for Breakfast

May 28, 2018

Yesterday, I have received feedback on my blog posts. One aspect of the feedback was “I would also like to start publishing, but I do not dare to share my work. You know, I am not as courageous as you are and cannot write such personal things…”

Which actually left me eyes-wide-open astonished. Not because I had only 3 blog posts with about 600 words in total. But because I do not think that courage has had anything to do with it. And I think: everyone can do that.

Come back with me one week and you will hopefully understand why:

Time warp one week back…

About a week ago, my inner dialogue was something like this:

  • If I start publishing, I want the result to be awesome
  • because I do not want other people to think ‘oh, no, not another one of those amateur blogs’

With that as a foundation, I would go on to tell myself:

  • but I do not really have something awesome to write about - why should somebody else care about my thoughts…
  • and writing in an awesome way requires that I spend a lot of time and work on it - am I really sure I want to do that?

As a result, I would think “oh, this is too hard”. It felt like this HUGE TASK. It scared me. I did not think I had a good chance of being successful. And therefore, I did not start to write. And then I felt bad, and because I somehow knew that publishing was important to me and that I did not know how to accomplish my goal.

You can believe me when I say that courageous was the last thing I felt at that point in time. Au contraire, mon ami. I felt I did not have courage because I did not do the scary HUGE TASK.

So when did I get courageous?

That’s a trick question. Because as I said above, I do not think that courage has had anything to do with it. I did not need courage.

Something else was needed: The decision to shrink the scary HUGE TASK into the easily doable thing. I was able to change the success criteria in such a way that I was very confident I could achieve my goal.

Actually I remember how thought: Well, that’s easy - I can do this.

And how did I do it?

I think it started with giving myself the “permission to suck”. And it helped that I defined success as publishing only 100 words.

And now my inner dialogue looks something like this:

  1. The most important thing is that I publish at least 100 words every workday
  2. I try my best to make those 100 words helpful to at least one person
  3. I try to help by showing where I struggled and how I could make progress
  4. I celebrate after publishing my 100+ words - because that’s the thing only I can control.

This new thought process puts me in the driver seat. I am in control to write 100 words and publish them. And I know when I tried to help one person.

It is easy now to feel successful. I mean, 100 words, come on, that’s basically a short email. And It can even be a sucky email. Why would I need courage for that?

Everyone can do that

I believe that everybody can go from “the person that does not have the courage to publish” to “the person who thinks publishing is easy’ in a very short period of time.

For me, it took only a couple of hours work and 3 blog posts with about 600 words.

The magic trick is: Make your objective smaller. So small you do not need courage any more. That’s how you can get started.

My recommendation: Set up a new habit:

Every time you think “I am afraid, I need more courage”,
stop yourself, and think “Easy eats Courage for Breakfast”
and ask yourself “What could be an easy step that takes me into this direction?”

Again, I am not asking you to believe me. I am asking you to believe your own experience. Try this for a couple of days - and let me know how it works for you via Twitter or any other way.
