Why I Think Traditional Planning Kills Motivation

Aug 17, 2018

“Where’s your 90 day plan that you commit to?” I was asked yesterday.

OK, I had said that I am going to bring a plan. So I hesitated. Did something else. Did not plan. I did not want to commit. But why?

Because, in the end, I did not want to feel like failure.

Because if I did not meet my goal, or plan, at the “committed upon” point in time - what would that mean?

So, I did not commit.

And I think I am right

Here’s why: I simply cannot “estimate” correctly when I am going to be “done”. First thing is: “Done” needs to be defined. Then: the question of “when are you going to reach ‘done’” depends on several factors that influence “time of completion”.

Here’s a list of the factors from the top of my head. Let’s illustrate this by “laying a brick wall that is 1m high and 20m long”.

  • (Estimated) amount of work: to reach “done”, a certain amount of work needs to be “worked”. The unit of work takes a certain amount of time. Let’s estimate 1m width of a brick wall 1m high takes 3 hours to complete.

  • (Estimated) amount of “capacity to do work”: Well, if I have only 1hr during week days (because my day job is something else) and on the weekend I have 3,5hrs on both days, then I have a “capacity” of 51+23,5 = 12hrs per week. That’s 4m of brick wall per week, of 5 weeks for 20m of brick wall.

And this is before unexpected stuff happens.

But unexpected stuff happens

The guy who is supposed to bring the bricks and mortar every week has a problem with the upstream delivery of mortar. Two weeks delay. My child gets sick, and therefore I cannot work on Friday and the weekend. Another week delay.

That’s 8 weeks instead of a 5 weeks. Great. And since I have committed to 5 weeks, my “accountability partner” (aka boss) will be asking: What’s happening? (”Are you not working hard enough?”, “Are you not good enough?”)

The list of “reasons” for the “delay” feels like an excuse. Even if I document every reason for a delay, the next round if discussion goes like (”Everybody knows that stuff happens - you should have planned for that in the first place” - and I hear “You are not good enough to plan”).

What makes the process even more complicated is the fact that I want to do stuff I have never done before. So, how good are my estimates going to be? Very likely: very far from reality. So, it’s even more likely that I am “off target”.

More likely I have the “discussion”.

Plus, what happens, if there is stuff on my list that I do not control? I.e. where there is not 100% probability of success? I do not know if the customer signs up. So, how can I commit to having 10 customers even I am not even sure if my offer is valuable enough so I get one person to buy?

Even more likely to have “the discussion”.

So, let’s try and look at it from a perspective of “what’s the stuff that I (directly) control?”

Time (my own)

  • I can control the amount of time I invest (within limits, of course)
  • In that time, I can control what I work on
  • In that time, I can try to control how “focused” I work on the task
  • In that time, I can try to control how “effectively” I work on the task

Money

  • I can “control” the amount of money I invest (within limits)
  • I can control what I use the money for (until it’s gone)
  • I can decide if I do it myself or if I get someone else to do it

Resources that I do not control directly

  • There also might be resources that are indirectly available to me. Those are mentioned in the book “A beautiful constraint” (chapter 5). But I am not going to go into detail here.

So, would could I commit to?

I could commit to the things that are under my direct control. That includes the time and the money I am going to invest.

What’s next?

Once I commit to budget for time & money, the next most important “lever” is the question of “what do I need/want to do next”?

At that stage, the best “input” is probably someone who has walked the path before.

Or that of other people who say: Why do you think you need that? What would happen, if you tried this instead of that? I think there might be a shortcut here - have you thought about that?

And that’s the discussion I want to have.
