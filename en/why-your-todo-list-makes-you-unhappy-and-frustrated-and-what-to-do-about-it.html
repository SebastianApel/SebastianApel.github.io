Why Your Todo-List Makes You Unhappy & Frustrated (and What To Do About It)

Aug 29, 2018

Conventional wisdom takes todo-lists for granted

This article is part of a series of articles on “Self Management” on the intersection of “Happyness & Productivity”.

Todo-Lists have been with me for most of my professional life. I have taken them for granted. I have believed “that’s how professionals & high achievers do it”. (Oh, yes, and keeping all the stuff I want to do in my head does not work either. So, writing it down is the way to go, right?)

And this was reinforced by “conventional wisdom”. Blogs write about todo-lists. There are apps who help you to “digitize” your todo-list. So who am I to doubt the mighty wisdom of the todo-List? Apparently that’s what you need when you want to achieve.

The todo-list will help me to be happy, right?

For me, the underlying (not really explicit) belief (or maybe it’s better to call it “hope”) was: Once I have achieved, then I will be happy.

And the todo-list was supposed to help me do this.

But happy did not come. Instead, unhappy & frustrated & overwhelmed came.

But: It cannot be the todo-list, right? It must be myself who is doing it wrong… I’m not achieving enough. No wonder I’m not happy.

That’s what I believed for a long time…

Looking more closely

I don’t know where the courage came from, but recently I find myself looking at things I have taken “for granted” in a new way.

So, I started to observe, and I realized the following:

  • At the end of the day, I often was unhappy and felt I did not make any progress. Especially if I looked at all the stuff that I did not manage to get into the day.

And another observation was:

  • When I tick item “off” my todo list, then I automatically have to look at all the items that are NOT ticked yet.

Trying something else

So, I have decided to do something else at the end of each day:

I am now writing an entry into my “Achievement and Reflection” journal (basically a text document that I add to at the end).

I reflect on the following four questions:

  • What did I create and/or ship and/or achieve today?
  • What did I learn today?
  • What do I want to repeat in the future?
  • What do I want to do differently in the future?

Am amazing improvement

To me, this has made a huge difference. I can now look at the day more objectively and go home happy :-) This then helps me to rest and relax better, which in turn improves my happiness and effectiveness the next day…

The hardest part for me was to establish the habbit - and I will go more into details in another post.
